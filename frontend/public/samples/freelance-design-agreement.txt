FICTIONAL FREELANCE DESIGN AGREEMENT

Portfolio example only. The parties and project below are fictional. This document is not legal advice.

This Freelance Design Agreement is made on 12 July 2026 between BrightPath Foods Private Limited (“Client”) and Aisha Verma (“Designer”).

1. PROJECT
Designer will create a visual identity package consisting of a primary logo, secondary logo, color palette, typography recommendations, business-card layout and social-media templates. Work will be delivered electronically in commonly used source and export formats.

2. SCHEDULE
Designer will provide initial concepts within fifteen business days after receiving the deposit and complete project information. Client delays in supplying content or feedback may extend the schedule. No final delivery deadline or formal delay-notice procedure is stated.

3. FEES
The total project fee is INR 120,000. Client will pay fifty percent when signing and fifty percent after delivery of final files. Taxes and approved third-party expenses are additional. The Agreement does not make delivery conditional on cleared final payment.

4. REVISIONS
Designer will provide revisions until Client is satisfied. The Agreement does not limit the number of rounds, distinguish corrections from new scope, set feedback deadlines, or establish hourly pricing for additional work.

5. CLIENT RESPONSIBILITIES
Client will provide accurate content, brand information, timely feedback and one authorized decision-maker. Client represents that it has permission to use all names, text, images and materials supplied to Designer.

6. REVIEW AND ACCEPTANCE
Client will review final files and notify Designer whether the work is satisfactory. No objective acceptance criteria, review deadline, deemed-acceptance mechanism, or detailed rejection-and-correction process applies.

7. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY
All rights in final approved designs transfer to Client when final files are delivered. Transfer is not conditioned on receipt of full payment. Designer retains ownership of unused concepts, working files, methods, tools and pre-existing materials.

8. THIRD-PARTY MATERIALS
The work may include fonts, stock images, software assets or other licensed materials. Client is responsible for continuing license charges after delivery. The Agreement does not require a written third-party-materials schedule or clearly allocate infringement responsibility for Client-supplied content.

9. PORTFOLIO USE
Designer may display completed work in a portfolio, website, award submission and social media after public launch. Client may request a reasonable delay for an unreleased campaign.

10. ORIGINALITY WARRANTY
Designer warrants that original portions of the final work are created by Designer. The warranty does not expressly exclude Client-supplied materials, approved third-party assets, fonts or stock content.

11. LATE PAYMENT
Late invoices may be subject to a service charge. The rate, grace period, service-suspension right, collection costs and maximum lawful amount are not specified.

12. CANCELLATION
Either party may cancel the project by written notice without a cancellation fee. The Agreement does not expressly require payment for completed but undelivered work, non-refundable reserved capacity, committed third-party costs or transition assistance.

13. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY
Neither party will be liable for indirect or consequential losses. Designer’s total liability will not exceed fees actually paid under this Agreement, except where a limitation is prohibited by law.

14. INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR
Designer is an independent contractor and is responsible for taxes, insurance, equipment and work methods. Nothing creates employment, partnership, agency or authority to bind Client.

15. CONFIDENTIALITY
Each party will protect non-public business and project information and use it only for the project. Public portfolio display permitted under Section 9 is not a confidentiality breach.

16. GENERAL
Changes must be agreed in writing. Neither party may assign this Agreement without consent. This Agreement is governed by the laws of Karnataka, India, and courts in Bengaluru have jurisdiction.
